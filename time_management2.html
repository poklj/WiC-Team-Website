Time Management

Do your most important work first

It sounds obvious, but this advice is often ignored. Completing your most important work first, even if it's unpleasant, ensures you'll have time for it.

Reduce your distractions

Leave your phone alone as much as possible, log out of things like Steam, and avoid social media. Distractions are everywhere, and avoiding them will improve your efficiency.

Give yourself breaks between tasks

Even a few minutes between tasks helps you refocus as well as come back to your work in a better mood

Don't get lazy on weekends

If you have the free time, it can be tempting to spend the weekend relaxing and enjoying yourself. Unfortunately, work can pile up quickly and procrastinating because of the weekend is still procrastinating

Focus on the idea, not the details

It's easy to focus on details and end up spending a lot more time than expected getting a detail just right. It's also easy for this to result in rushed, late, or missed work. Focusing on getting your main ideas down first and then improving details as needed helps prevent this.
